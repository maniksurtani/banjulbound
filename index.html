What this is about?

Every year, resourceful and highly spirited adventure-loving kooks all over the world come together and form groups that will go on an epic voyage across Europe and West Africa. In a car worth just a few hundred pounds/euros they will drive from Plymouth, England to Banjul, the Gambia in pursuit of adventure, fortune, glory, raising money for good causes and perhaps have a some fun encounters along the way that may involve lions. If (and that is a big IF) they manage to get across Europe and through the rugged terrain of Africa without being eaten by lions, what is left of the car (after it survives the 3,700 mile/5,920 km journey) is flogged to the highest bidder in an auction in Banjul, with proceeds going to charity.

Why are we doing this?

Why did Mallory and Irvine attempt to climb Everest? Why did Amundsen and Scott race to the south pole? Why did Justin Esch and Dave Lefkow go beyond bacon-salt to invent Baconnaise? Because it was a damn cool thing to do and the result was awesome!

Okay, more realistically, it's to raise money for ActionAid, our chosen charity. ActionAid focuses on the people that others forget. People in poverty. People who face discrimination. People whose voices are ignored.
